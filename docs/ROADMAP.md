## Feature Prioritization Framework

### How We Decide What to Build

**1. Customer Impact (40%)**
- How many customers will use this?
- How much will it improve their results?
- Will it reduce churn?

**2. Business Impact (30%)**
- Does it increase revenue (new customers or upsells)?
- Does it reduce costs?
- Does it build competitive moat?

**3. Effort (20%)**
- How long will it take to build?
- What's the technical complexity?
- Do we have the skills in-house?

**4. Strategic Alignment (10%)**
- Does it align with long-term vision?
- Does it differentiate us from competitors?
- Does it create network effects?

**Scoring:**
- Each criterion scored 1-10
- Weighted average calculated
- Prioritize features scoring 7.5+

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks

**Risk: AI costs too high**
- **Mitigation:** Implement caching, use smaller models for simple tasks, negotiate volume discounts with OpenAI

**Risk: Platform downtime**
- **Mitigation:** Multi-region deployment, automated failover, 99.9% SLA, on-call rotations

**Risk: Security breach**
- **Mitigation:** SOC 2 compliance, regular penetration testing, bug bounty program, encryption at rest & in transit

---

### Product Risks

**Risk: Feature bloat (too complex)**
- **Mitigation:** Ruthless prioritization, user testing before launch, "less is more" philosophy

**Risk: Poor AI quality**
- **Mitigation:** Continuous prompt engineering, user feedback loops, human-in-the-loop for critical tasks

**Risk: Slow product velocity**
- **Mitigation:** Weekly sprint cycles, automate testing, reduce technical debt, hire senior engineers

---

### Market Risks

**Risk: Competitor copies us**
- **Mitigation:** Build moat through proprietary data, network effects, brand, move fast

**Risk: Market saturation**
- **Mitigation:** Expand internationally, move upmarket (enterprise), add new verticals (B2B, services)

**Risk: Economic downturn**
- **Mitigation:** Focus on ROI (we save money vs. tool stack), offer flexible pricing, maintain low burn rate
